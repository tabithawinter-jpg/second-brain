# Function Inventory

> Use this file in Day 2 of the bootcamp ("Audit the team you already are"). The skill pre-seeds the template; you fill in the rows.

## How to use this

Two passes. Take ten minutes per pass.

**Pass 1. List every function you currently perform.** Don't filter. Don't merge. Don't editorialise. Write each one as a short verb phrase ("write proposals", "manage invoicing", "post on LinkedIn", "handle client onboarding"). Stop at twenty if you get there fast; aim for at least ten.

**Pass 2. Categorise each row.** For every function, mark which category it fits:

- **Fully externalisable**: could be done entirely by an AI employee or external contractor with the right brief, without you in the loop.
- **Partial**: needs your input or sign-off but the work can be drafted, prepared, or scaffolded by someone else. The 80% Principle territory.
- **Founder IP**: only you can do this. The thing that is the business, not just inside the business.

If you're between categories, lean toward the more delegable one. The audit reveals where time can be freed, not where the comfort is.

## What to do with the audit

The rows you mark **Fully externalisable** are the first candidates for an AI employee's job description. They feed straight into Q3 of `/hire` ("What are the 3-5 things this employee will do for you?").

The rows you mark **Partial** are the second wave. They become the work you do faster once your AI employee is up and running.

The rows you mark **Founder IP** stay with you. The whole point is to clear your time so you can do more of these.
